# Quality Systems Portfolio

Professional portfolio documenting practical work in Quality Management Systems (QMS), laboratory quality assurance, quality control, regulatory compliance, risk management, continuous improvement, and applied quality analytics across laboratory, manufacturing, food, beverage, pharmaceutical, and public health environments.

> **Purpose:** To provide verifiable evidence of technical capability through documented quality projects, audit documentation, quality methodologies, compliance frameworks, and evidence-based process improvement.

---

## Repository Overview

This repository forms part of a structured technical portfolio demonstrating the practical implementation of Quality Management Systems across laboratory, industrial, manufacturing, healthcare, and research environments.

The portfolio is designed to showcase professional competence through documented work rather than unsupported claims. Every project included demonstrates practical problem-solving, quality management principles, scientific documentation, regulatory compliance, risk-based thinking, and continuous improvement.

---

## Repository Objectives

This repository aims to:

* Demonstrate Quality Management System implementation through practical projects.
* Document laboratory quality assurance and quality control methodologies.
* Showcase regulatory compliance and quality documentation.
* Present audit readiness, CAPA, and process improvement initiatives.
* Publish quality manuals, templates, SOPs, and case studies.
* Integrate quality management with modern analytical tools.
* Maintain a growing record of continuous professional development in quality systems.

## Standards and Good Practices

Projects and documentation are developed with reference to internationally recognised quality management principles where applicable, including:

* ISO/IEC 17025 laboratory quality principles
* ISO 9001 quality management principles
* Good Laboratory Practice (GLP)
* Good Manufacturing Practice (GMP)
* Hazard Analysis and Critical Control Points (HACCP)
* Corrective and Preventive Action (CAPA)
* Risk-based thinking
* Continuous improvement

Where standards are referenced, they are used to support learning and demonstrate best practices. This repository does not reproduce proprietary standards.
